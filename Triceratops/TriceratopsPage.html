Triceratops

When

-The triceratops was discovered in 1887.

-Charles Marsh is the one who discovered it.

-It was believed to have lived in the late cretaceous period.

-Lived about 68-66 million years ago.

-Located in North America.

Appearance

-Paleontologists believe the triceratops was about 9m long.

-The largest adults are thought to have weighed between 5,000 and 7,000kg

-They are known for having 3 horns, 2 over the eyes and one on the snout.

Diet

-Triceratops were herbivores.

-They only ate vegetation.

-They would eat ferns, cycads, and cinifers.

-They could eat about 100-300 pounds of vegetation daily.

-Thats like eating 1,000 lettuce heads in a single day.

ANATOMY

The Skeleton

-Triceratops have approximately 206 bones.

-The full skeleton was first put together in the 1900s.

-The largest skeleton is kept in the Glazer Childrens Museum in Tampa, Florida.

-It is 66 million years old and has been named Big John.

-It measures 10 feet tall and 26 feet long.
