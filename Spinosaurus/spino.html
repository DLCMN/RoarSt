Spinosaurs



Diet:carnivorous.

Teeth:smooth and cone-shaped.

Food:fish and maybe other dinosaurs.

When it lived: Late Cretaceous, 99-94 million years ago.

Found in:Egypt,Morocco

Type of dinosaur:Large theropod

Length:14.0m

Weight:7400kg

The Spinosaurus was a large predatory dinosaur native to Africa, and current research suggests it may have been semi-aquatic. For over a century, scientific knowledge about this species remained limited due to the scarcity of complete fossils.
The first bones discovered in 1912 were later destroyed in World War II, further complicating efforts to study the dinosaur.

Apperance:

For many decades, scientists pictured Spinosaurus as a land-dwelling dinosaur with long back legs, similar to dinosaurs like Tyrannosaurus and Allosaurus.
However, new discoveries show that it actually had much shorter legs and probably spent a lot of time in or near the water. Its tail was also broad and shaped like a paddle. Researchers still aren't sure if Spinosaurus was an active swimmer or if it mainly stayed along the water's edge.
